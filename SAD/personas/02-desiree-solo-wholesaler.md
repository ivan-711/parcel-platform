# Persona 02: Desiree — The Solo Wholesaler

## 1. Profile Summary

**Name:** Desiree Thompson
**Age:** 31
**Location:** Memphis, TN metro area
**Education:** B.A. Communications, University of Memphis (2017)
**Household:** Single, no kids, lives alone in a 2BR apartment in Midtown. Has a younger sister she's trying to get into wholesaling. Drives a 2022 Honda Accord she paid cash for — her first "proof of concept" purchase after a big assignment fee.

**Background:** Desiree spent four years in pharmaceutical sales after college. She was good at it — top 15% in her region — but hated the ceiling. She discovered wholesaling through a BiggerPockets podcast in early 2023, closed her first deal three months later, and quit her W-2 by October of that year. She hasn't looked back.

**Investment Experience:** 2.5 years full-time wholesaling. Has closed roughly 70 deals total. She understands ARV, repair estimates, and the 70% rule cold. She can comp a property in 12 minutes. She has dabbled in the idea of keeping a rental but has never actually held a property — every deal has been an assignment or double close.

**Income:** $100,000–$140,000/year from assignment fees averaging $8,000–$15,000 per deal. She closes 2–4 deals per month, with seasonal variation (Q1 is slow, Q3 is her best quarter). Her best single month was $47,000. Her worst month in the last year was $4,200 — one deal, low margin, rough seller negotiation.

**Investment Strategy:** Wholesale-dominant, but increasingly curious about keeping one property per quarter as a BRRRR or buy-and-hold. She has $38,000 in savings earmarked for her first rental but hasn't pulled the trigger because she doesn't have a system to evaluate hold vs. assign decisions side-by-side.

**Personality Traits:** Extremely organized. Runs her day off a color-coded Google Calendar. Competitive — she tracks her KPIs in a spreadsheet every Sunday night. Impatient with software that wastes her time. She'll pay for good tools but resents paying for five of them. Prefers texting over calling. Night owl — does her follow-up campaigns between 9 PM and midnight.

## 3. Detailed Daily/Weekly Workflow

### Monday — List Building & Campaign Launch

**8:00 AM** — Opens PropStream on her laptop. Filters Memphis metro: absentee owners, equity >40%, owned 10+ years, SFR only. Exports a CSV of ~400 records.

**8:45 AM** — Opens the CSV in Google Sheets. Manually removes duplicates against her master "Do Not Contact" sheet (previous opt-outs and closed deals). This takes 20 minutes.

**9:15 AM** — Uploads the cleaned list to Launch Control. Writes 3 SMS templates (she A/B tests constantly). Schedules the campaign for 10 AM send.

**9:45 AM** — Opens REsimpli. Filters pipeline by "Follow-Up Due Today." There are 23 leads. She clicks into each one, reads her last notes, decides whether to call or text. For the ones she'll text, she switches to her phone and sends messages through Launch Control so they come from her marketing number.

**11:00 AM** — First responses start coming in from today's SMS campaign. She gets a "Who is this?" reply in Launch Control. She responds. Two minutes later, a "Yes, I'm interested" reply comes in. She copies the property address from Launch Control, opens PropStream, pulls the property details, then opens REsimpli and creates a new lead manually — entering the address, owner name, phone number, estimated ARV, and her notes from the text conversation. This process takes 8–12 minutes per interested lead.

**12:00 PM** — Lunch. Scrolls TikTok for 20 minutes, checks her wholesale Facebook group for buyer posts.

**1:00 PM** — Appointment block. She has two seller appointments today (phone calls — she rarely does in-person until she has a verbal agreement). Opens DealCheck on her phone during the call, punches in numbers while the seller talks. After each call, she goes back to REsimpli and updates the lead status and notes.

**3:00 PM** — Admin block. Updates her Google Sheets KPI tracker with today's numbers: leads generated, conversations started, appointments set, offers made. Cross-references against REsimpli to make sure nothing slipped through.

**4:30 PM** — She has a deal under contract closing this week. Opens DocuSign to check that the assignment contract was signed by her buyer. It was. She screenshots the signed doc and uploads it to REsimpli's file section for that lead. Then she emails her title company the assignment agreement.

### Wednesday — Driving for Dollars

**4:00 PM** — Drives three target zip codes in South Memphis. Opens DealMachine on her phone (mounted on dashboard). When she spots a distressed property — boarded windows, overgrown lawn, code violation notice — she taps the property on the map. DealMachine pulls the owner info. She adds a tag and a voice note: "Blue house, caving roof, mail piled up, neighbor said owner moved to Atlanta."

**6:30 PM** — Back home. Opens DealMachine on her laptop, exports the 14 properties she tagged today as a CSV. Opens REsimpli, imports them as new leads. Three of them fail to import because of address formatting mismatches. She fixes those manually.

**7:00 PM** — Takes the same 14 addresses and creates a skip trace batch through REsimpli's built-in skip trace (which charges per record). Gets back phone numbers for 11 of the 14. Adds those to her next Launch Control SMS campaign.

### Friday — Disposition & Deal Management

**9:00 AM** — She has a property under contract at $65,000 that she needs to assign. Opens her Google Sheets buyer list. Filters by "buy box includes South Memphis" and "budget $80K+." She has 12 matching buyers. She manually copies the property details from REsimpli, pastes them into a text blast she sends from her personal phone to all 12 buyers.

**10:30 AM** — Three buyers are interested. She schedules property access for two of them. The third wants more details — she opens PropStream to pull recent comps, screenshots them, and texts the images.

**2:00 PM** — Buyer #2 agrees to $82,000. Her assignment fee is $17,000. She opens DocuSign, sends the assignment contract, then updates REsimpli from "Under Contract" to "Assigned." Updates her Google Sheets deal log with the fee amount and expected closing date.

### Sunday — Weekly Review

**8:00 PM** — Opens Google Sheets. Updates weekly KPIs: total leads in pipeline (147), new leads this week (47), offers made (6), contracts signed (1), deals closed (2), revenue ($23,500). Compares to last week and last month. Calculates her lead-to-close conversion rate (2.8%) and cost-per-deal.

**9:00 PM** — Plans next week. Decides which lists to pull, which follow-up sequences need attention, which deals need contract extensions.

## 4. Pain Points (Ranked)

### P1: Data Re-Entry Across Five Disconnected Tools (Critical)

**What:** Every lead touches 3–5 tools during its lifecycle: PropStream (sourcing) → Launch Control (first contact) → REsimpli (pipeline) → DealCheck (analysis) → DocuSign (contracts). None of them talk to each other. She manually copies property addresses, owner names, phone numbers, ARVs, and notes between apps dozens of times per day.

**Why it exists:** She assembled her stack tool-by-tool as she grew, optimizing each function independently. No single platform existed that did all of it well enough.

**What it costs:** 30–45 minutes per lead in redundant data entry. At 80–120 new leads per week, this is 40–90 hours/month — roughly 25–35% of her working hours are spent on data janitoring, not revenue-generating activities. She estimates she's losing 1–2 deals per month because leads go cold during the lag between first contact and pipeline entry.

**How Parcel solves it:** Single platform from list pull through skip trace, first contact, pipeline management, analysis, and contract tracking. A lead that responds to an SMS campaign is automatically in the CRM with full property data attached. Zero re-entry.

### P2: No Way to Evaluate "Keep vs. Assign" Decisions (High)

**What:** She has $38,000 saved to buy her first rental, but every deal gets wholesaled because she has no system to compare assignment profit against long-term hold returns. DealCheck does basic rental math, but it's not connected to her pipeline — she'd have to re-enter every property's data to run a hold analysis.

**Why it exists:** Her CRM (REsimpli) is wholesale-only. It doesn't model rental income, cash-on-cash return, or BRRRR scenarios. She'd need a separate tool and workflow to evaluate hold strategies.

**What it costs:** She has wholesaled at least 3 properties in the past year that she now believes would have been better as rentals — properties in appreciating neighborhoods with strong rent-to-price ratios. Estimated missed wealth-building: $40,000–$80,000 in equity she'll never capture.

**How Parcel solves it:** Multi-strategy calculators let her run wholesale, BRRRR, buy-and-hold, and creative finance analyses on the same deal from the same pipeline screen. The AI deal narration can proactively flag: "This property's rent-to-price ratio is 1.2%. Have you considered a BRRRR analysis? Here's what it looks like."

### P3: SMS and CRM Are on Separate Islands (High)

**What:** Launch Control handles all her outbound SMS campaigns and inbound replies, but none of that conversation history lives in REsimpli. When a seller replies "Yes, I'm interested," Desiree has to manually create the lead in her CRM and type up a summary of the text conversation. If she forgets, the lead falls through.

**Why it exists:** Launch Control is a standalone SMS platform. REsimpli has basic texting but it's unreliable and lacks the deliverability and compliance features of a dedicated SMS tool.

**What it costs:** She estimates 5–8 leads per month slip through because she doesn't transfer them fast enough. At her conversion rate (2.8%) and average fee ($11,000), that's $1,540–$2,464/mo in potential lost revenue.

**How Parcel solves it:** Wave 4 native SMS/email comms with drip sequences. Every text conversation lives on the contact record. A seller's reply automatically triggers pipeline stage changes and task creation.

### P4: No Intelligent Follow-Up Prioritization (Medium)

**What:** She has 147 leads in her pipeline. Every morning, she manually scrolls through REsimpli's follow-up list and decides who to contact based on gut feel and whatever notes she left last time. There's no scoring, no prioritization, no AI-assisted triage.

**Why it exists:** REsimpli's follow-up system is date-based only — it tells her who's "due" for follow-up but not who's most likely to convert or who needs attention most urgently.

**What it costs:** She spends 45–60 minutes every morning on triage. More importantly, she's allocating equal time to cold leads and warm leads because she has no signal beyond her own memory. High-intent sellers who need a same-day callback sometimes wait 48 hours because they're buried in the list.

**How Parcel solves it:** AI deal narration and morning briefing. Parcel surfaces the 5 leads most likely to move today, explains why ("Seller mentioned a tax lien deadline in your last conversation — that's 11 days away"), and suggests specific actions. The morning briefing replaces her manual triage entirely.

### P5: Monthly Software Spend Is Unsustainable at Scale (Medium)

**What:** She's paying $472–$500+/mo across five tools, and if she scales to 5–6 deals/month she'll need higher tiers on most of them. Launch Control alone could hit $300/mo at higher volume. Her projected spend at 2x volume: $700–$900/mo.

**Why it exists:** Each tool is priced independently and optimized for its own vertical. There's no bundle discount for using the ecosystem because there is no ecosystem.

**What it costs:** The dollar amount itself isn't crippling at her income level, but the cost scales linearly with volume while her revenue scales with conversion rate improvements — which these tools don't help with. She's paying more without getting smarter.

**How Parcel solves it:** Parcel Pro at $79/mo replaces REsimpli ($149), DealCheck (free but limited), and DealMachine ($49) — a $198/mo savings on day one. As Waves 4–6 ship (SMS, skip tracing, direct mail), she drops Launch Control ($150) and standalone skip trace costs. Projected Parcel-only spend: $79/mo + usage-based add-ons, estimated $120–$160/mo total at her current volume. Savings: $300–$350/mo.

## 8. Parcel's Voice for This Persona

### Tone Principles for Desiree

Desiree is competent, fast, and metrics-driven. She doesn't need hand-holding on wholesale fundamentals — she knows what ARV, MAO, and assignment fees are. She needs Parcel's AI to be a **sharp, concise analytical partner** that surfaces insights she'd miss and challenges her default assumptions (which default to "wholesale it").

- **Be direct.** She hates fluff. Don't say "You might want to consider..." Say "This property looks better as a hold. Here's why."
- **Lead with numbers.** She thinks in dollars and percentages. Every insight should have a number attached.
- **Challenge the default.** Her instinct is to wholesale everything. Parcel's job is to make her pause when the hold economics are compelling.
- **Respect her speed.** She moves fast. Insights should be scannable in under 5 seconds.

**Insight 1 — Pipeline Prioritization (Morning Briefing)**
> "You have 23 follow-ups due today. Three are high-priority: the seller at **1847 Lamar** mentioned a probate court date — that's 9 days out, and motivated sellers move fast near deadlines. **2203 Chelsea** hasn't responded to your last 2 texts but opened both — try a call. **944 National** has been in your pipeline for 38 days with no offer — either make one today or archive it."

**Insight 2 — Strategy Challenge (Deal Analysis)**
> "Your MAO on **3411 Faxon** is $62,000 with a projected assignment fee of $13,000. But this property is in a zip code where rents rose 11% last year. As a BRRRR: $925/mo rent, 15.8% cash-on-cash, and you'd recoup your $38K down payment in equity within 18 months. Your assignment fee equals 7 months of cash flow. Want to see the full BRRRR breakdown?"

**Insight 3 — Campaign Performance (SMS Analytics)**
> "Your Tuesday pre-foreclosure list had a 4.7% response rate — that's 2x your absentee owner campaigns. Three of the 6 respondents mentioned they need to sell within 60 days. Your average time-to-close on motivated pre-foreclosure leads is 19 days. Consider doubling your pre-foreclosure list volume next week."

For a **beginner investor** running the same analysis on 3411 Faxon, Parcel would say:

For **Desiree**, the same information is compressed:

> "Assignment fee: $13K. But the BRRRR numbers are stronger — 15.8% CoC, $925/mo rent, equity recoup in 18 months. Want the full breakdown?"

The difference: no definitions, no hand-holding, just data and a nudge.
